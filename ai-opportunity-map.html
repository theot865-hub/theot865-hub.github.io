The Source
Anthropic's 2024 Labour Market Study mapped AI's theoretical capability against observed usage across every major occupation — 52 million job postings analysed. The gap between blue and red is what you're seeing. It is enormous. And it is almost entirely unclaimed.
When the blue gets filled
Enterprise AI is moving fast. Here's how long the window stays open by sector.
1–2 yrs
Legal · Finance · Office & Admin · Computer & Math
High document volume, clear ROI, enterprise vendors already deployed. Window closing fast.
3–5 yrs
Healthcare · Education · Sales · Arts & Media
Regulation, trust, and workflow complexity are slowing adoption — but not stopping it.
5–10 yrs
Trades · Construction · Food & Serving · Agriculture
Physical industries. Enterprise won't touch them for years. This is the small business window.

Where the entrepreneur wins
Enterprise moves slowly at the bottom of the market. The small business operator who moves first in their local sector has a 3–7 year window before they face real competition.
Local trades & construction
No enterprise vendor is building for the 4-person plumbing company. First mover advantage is massive and uncontested.
Independent healthcare operators
Private clinics, physio practices, and allied health are completely ignored by enterprise AI. Huge admin burden. Huge opportunity.
Franchise & multi-location food
Operations, inventory, and staff scheduling are still manual. The operator who automates first runs leaner than everyone else in their market.
SMB professional services
Solo accountants, local lawyers, independent consultants — they have the same document burden as enterprise but none of the tools. Yet.
